Things I've Changed My Mind About

Experience quietly changes assumptions. A few things I think differently about today:

01. Traffic growth

I used to think more traffic was always better. Now I know that 100 targeted visitors are worth more than 10,000 "info-seekers" who will never convert.

"Acquisition quality matters as much as volume."

02. SEO problems

SEO problems often aren't SEO problems, i used to see SEO as a siloed set of tasks. Now I see them as business problems—revenue leaks, user friction, and brand visibility gaps.

"Many eventually turn out to be reporting, ownership, or process issues."

03. Internal linking

I used to treat internal links as a crawl efficiency play. Now I see them as a high-leverage product feature that defines the user's journey through our knowledge base.

"Over time I stopped seeing it as a checklist task and started treating it like a growth lever."

04. Broad Search

Search is becoming broader than search engines. I used to focus solely on the SERP. Now I look at the entire ecosystem—Reddit, YouTube, AI overviews—and ask where the conversation is happening.

"Visibility increasingly happens outside traditional SERPs."
